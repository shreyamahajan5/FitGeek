  • Stress Determinator

    The Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) is a renowned tool used for assessing stress levels. Originally developed in 1983, it remains a prominent choice for understanding the impact of various situations on individuals' perceived stress. Through a series of questions regarding feelings and thoughts over the last month, the PSS aims to gauge stress perception. Each question prompts respondents to indicate the frequency of certain emotions or thoughts, emphasizing quick and instinctive responses. While some questions may seem similar, they capture distinct aspects, warranting separate consideration. The key is to provide estimates rather than precise counts, offering valuable insights into stress perception and its nuances.
